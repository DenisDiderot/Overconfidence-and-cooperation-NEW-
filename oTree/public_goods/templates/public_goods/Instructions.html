Instructions

This second part of the experiment is in the tradition of the public good games, with the difference that the return of the group project, called alpha, will depend on the relative ability shown either by you or by your group companion in Task 1. You are going to play this game for 12 consecutive rounds, one of which will be relevant for the payment of this task. In every round you are going to face a single opponent, randomly drawn at the beginning of each round, and the return (alpha) can change as well. If in a given round alpha depends on your ability, you will be informed about this, however you will not receive any information about the value of your ability. In case it will depend on the ability of the other, you will also receive some information about his performance.

In each round of this Task you are going to be endowed with 100 points that you can allocate either in a shared project or keep it for yourself (every combination is possible, please choose integer numbers only). Each player’s payoff at the end of the round would be the sum of the amount of money kept for himself plus a share of the common project. This works as follows: both players invest part of their money in it, the sum of them is multiplied by a factor alpha and then the result is returned to both player.
